Introduction

It is six months since the conclusion of Operation Solstice Rain. Captain Amanda Farris has been rescued, and the Vestan Sovereignty Armed Forces (VSAF) have been ejected from Nov Elysia, temporarily stalling the Vestan takeover of Cressidium. With the UNS-CV Rio Grande in orbit and Captain Farris reinstated, Union Naval Command has authorized limited engagement of Union Armed Forces in a true combat capacity. Our objective is to aid the LSA against the newly emergent threat of the NVRG, which has been launching terror raids into LSA and Okasnian territories ever since their hostile takeover of the Vestan Military Command.

Situation

The NVRG coup, orchestrated by cloneborne Anthrochauvinist SecCom officers and their proteges, has destabilized the Vestian Sovereignty, Cressidium's largest nation-state. This has caused economic decline in the Republic of Okasnia and similarly affected the LSA. The UNS-CV Rio Grande, under Captain Farris and Alex Kim, is mediating between the LSA and the few remaining Loyalist Vestan Armed Forces (LVAF), pitting them against the NVRG.

While small, the LVAF are well-trained and, alongside two Union Lancer detachments, represent the only mechanized forces capable of expeditionary action into Okasnia and Vesta. The NVRG, though a fraction of the former VSAF, remains superior to combined LSA and Okasnian forces. Complicating matters, the NVRG has contracted well-equipped mercenary companies, some with Union-spec technology, who should be considered extremely dangerous. GMS support is arriving slowly, with Central Command hesitant to allocate more resources without a higher threat level.

Campaign Objectives

  • Primary: Capture, if possible, ranking members of the New Vestan Revolutionary Guard (NVRG) for Union interrogation.
  • Primary: Verify the status of Ambassador Nilan Bannerjee. If alive, arrange a priority rescue.
  • Secondary: Neutralize the NVRG high command structure.
  • Tertiary: Enact the dispersion of local Vestan Warlords and entrenched mercenary commanders. Pursue peaceful dispersion first; apply military force at Lancer team discretion if necessary.
  • Strategic Goal: Stabilize the territory of the former Vestan Sovereignty to facilitate a Union diplomatic mission aimed at establishing egalitarian civil structures and lasting planetary peace, furthering Cressidium's path to Core-world status.

Current Mission Parameters & Docket

Operation Black Tide Breaker represents the first incursion of Union forces into Vesta, with Mission Codename Fireflight laying the groundwork for a beachhead in northern Okasnia, which is occupied currently by Revolutionary Guard forces. From their fortified position on the island of Indalore, the NVRG have launched non-stop terror campaigns against the Okasnians, aimed at keeping their naval and air forces occupied and unable to strike into the more vulnerable positions of the Vestan mainland.

With the LSAA still recoiling from the devastation of Nov Elysia, the Leandric states have thus far been unable to provide relief for the Okasnians, a nominal trading partner and former Sovereignty tributary. When the Sovereignty fell after the failure to capture the LSA high command, the Okasnians broke off and were met with swift retribution by the newly formed NVRG. The attacks into the archipelago have not stopped, with the superior NVRG raiding forces harrying and chipping away at the outdated Okasnian militia and regular forces.

Historical Context

Union Naval Intelligence Officer Mayra Strong's intel reveals NVRG commanders are generational prodigies of Anthrochauvinist SecCom officers who fled Union Space over two hundred years ago, after the fall of the Second Committee. Intelligence reveals that at least a few of their number are generational, vat-grown clones of known SecCom war-criminals, with at least one being identified as a lead orchestrator of the Hercynian Crisis.

Their invasion plans were expedited by the arrival of IPS-N, GMS, and Union presence in the Cressidium system, creating conflict between the old Anthrochauvanist guard and the former Vestan Royal Family and their progressive VSAF elements.

The Current Ground Situation

The UNS-CV Rio Grande intercepted several mercenary companies, but others successfully landed and are under NVRG contract; these units possess Union-specification technology, training, and cybernetics, rendering them highly dangerous. Among them are The Bad Suns and Rogue Planet free companies. Force composition reports are still being tabulated.

Retrieval of Critical Personnel

Ambassador Nilan Bannerjee, a high-ranking Union diplomat, was not rescued during Operation Solstice Rain. Their considerable diplomatic skills and familiarity with Cressidium's cultures make them potentially instrumental in future peace negotiations if they are recovered alive. REDACTED PENDING REVIEW: There are no credible leads currently as to their whereabouts, though it is believed they have been moved to a secure facility in either Vestan occupied Okasnia or is being held in a Vestan political prisoner camp further inland. Additional research into their location will need to be performed before any retrieval action can be taken.

The Missing Vestan Royals

It is currently unknown whether or not the royal family has been completely culled by the NVRG new command or if they have been moved or imprisoned. If the Royal Family still exists, they may be a useful political alternative to the NVRG, who are seen in the outskirts of the old Sovereignty as oppressive and draconian. Further intelligence will need to be gathered to determine the viability of this effort.

OpFor Anlysis

New Vestan Revolutionary Guard Corps

The New Vestan Revolutionary Guard is the reorganized remains of the former VSAF. Their force numbers are still not well understood, but in the aftermath of the purge of the progressive elements of the former VSAF, the consolidated forces seem to be largely composed of Vestan special forces and veteran corps.

It is likely that the most senior elements of the Vestan military removed potentially problematic officers and elements prior to the coup, allowing for the orchestrators to grab the most advanced and powerful hardware the former VSAF had on standby. While their reduced numbers has limited their ability to occupy large swathes of territory, it has not reduced their offensive capabilities meaningfully.

Tactical Profile

The NVRG composition is a balanced mix of largely medium and light frames. Their role in this conflict is generalist, which has mandated that their fabrication supply chain has been largely focused on providing a strong frontline. While the former VSAF fielded a very wide variety of mechs, the current composition the NVRG are providing to the war effort are close-to-medium range, bolstered by a strong aerial contingent.

The Bad Suns GmbH

The Bad Suns is a mercenary company well known to Union. They have made a vast fortune by opposing Union expansion in the frontier worlds, allying with the Aun, the Karrakins and now the NVRG to stymie Union recontact endeavors in the outer rim.

They are able to field several hundred mech chassis across their multiple theaters of operation, and their numbers on Cressidium are estimated to be somewhere between thirty and forty chassis, which indicates a significant investment by the company and a significant payout by the NVRG. Their equipment is well kept, and they utilize frames from smaller, local manufacturers, as well as some from more reputable and well known foundries under the Union umbrella. Though technically few in number, their capabilities should not be underestimated.

Tactical Profile

The Bad Suns field an extensive array of high powered, high quality chassis piloted by veterans of multiple conflicts. As a result, forces deployed by the Bad Suns tend to be smaller in number than either the NVRG or Rogue Planet contingents, but much higher in lethality per unit fielded. They are often times employed in mixed formations with both NVRG and Rogue Planet forces, though almost always in a commanderie or auxiliary capacity.

Rogue Planet Corp

A relatively new contender in the privatized warfare sector, Rogue Planet operates on a much larger scale than most other mercenary companies. Rogue Planet fields great swathes of automated chaff units that form a vast frontline to protect backline officers and controllers.

Their "flood the zone" strategy makes them an excellent battering ram and invasion force, their automated armies more than capable of following simple "Capture and Hold" directives. Where a hammer is needed, the Rogue Planet excels, but they are not entirely without precision. Their backline Command and Control detachments not only oversee the direction of their frontline forces, but also field cadres of capable electronic warfare units. When fielded behind their veritable body-shield of metal grunts, these specialists can be used like a scalpel, attacking both infrastructure and enemy systems to great effect.

Tactical Profile

Rogue Planet relies on raw quantity to form a frontline to protect their flesh and bones specialists on the back line. They are known for fielding above average amount of COMP/CON and remote piloted chassis - easily replaced and repaired after being disabled. Their backline is comprised of artillery, e-warfare specialists and long-range chassis, which make offensive manuevers safely from behind their shield of metal.
